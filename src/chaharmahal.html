Chaharmahal-Bakhtiari province

Chaharmahal and Bakhtiari Province, located in western Iran, is a mountainous region known for its breathtaking natural landscapes, rivers, and rich nomadic culture. It is home to the Bakhtiari people, one of the most famous Iranian nomadic groups, whose traditions, lifestyle, and music strongly reflect a deep connection with nature and tribal identity. The region has preserved many of its ancient customs, making it an important center of Iranian ethnic heritage.

One of the most distinctive cultural expressions of this region is Lori (Bakhtiari) dance, a powerful and rhythmic group dance performed especially at celebrations, weddings, and social gatherings. The dance is characterized by synchronized steps, shoulder movements, and circular formations, symbolizing unity, courage, and community strength. It is often accompanied by traditional instruments such as the sorna and dohol, which create a strong and energetic rhythm that guides the dancers.

In addition to its rich dance tradition, the region is also known for its craftsmanship in brass work. Brass objects—ranging from decorative vessels to household items—are traditionally hand-shaped and engraved with intricate patterns. This craft reflects both practicality and artistic expression, showcasing geometric and floral designs that are deeply rooted in Iranian decorative traditions.
