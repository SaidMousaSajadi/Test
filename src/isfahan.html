Isfahan city

Isfahan, located in central Iran, is one of the most important historical and cultural cities of the Persian world. Often described as “half of the world” due to its beauty and architectural richness, the city is renowned for its Islamic architecture, historic bridges, and vibrant artistic traditions that flourished especially during the Safavid era. Isfahan has long been a major center of Persian art, craft, and culture.

One of its most famous traditional crafts is Khatamkari, a delicate form of marquetry in which tiny pieces of wood, bone, and metal are carefully arranged into intricate geometric patterns to decorate objects such as boxes, frames, and furniture. Another iconic art of the region is Termeh, a luxurious handwoven textile known for its rich colors and elegant patterns inspired by Persian motifs.

Among the most refined decorative techniques in Isfahan is Sermeh Doozi, an ancient embroidery style that uses metallic threads to create detailed and elegant designs, often applied on Termeh, velvet, or silk fabrics. This art has historically been associated with luxury and ceremonial use.

Another important motif in Persian textile and design culture is Boteh Jegheh, a teardrop-shaped pattern that symbolizes life, eternity, and growth, and is widely used in carpets, fabrics, and decorative arts throughout Iran.
